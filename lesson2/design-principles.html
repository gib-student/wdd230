Visual Hierarchy

CVS Health

cvshealth.com

CVS is a company concerned about the physical health of their customers, and so it is appropriate that on their homepage they show pictures of family members loving one another, and being happy and healthy. It suggests that their company will help you to live a happy and healthy life. These photos span the entire width of the screen, making them visually stand out. Additionally, there are big words superimposed over the photos which tell the viewers their mission and company promise.

White Space and Clean Space

Apple

apple.com

Between the photos, text, links and other things present on the Apple webpage, there is lots of space. This empty space is effective at causing the things on the site which do occupy space really pop out to the eyes of the viewer. They realy send the message: "We have big ideas here at Apple, and we care about simplicity." That is what all of their products are centered on: simplicity and user-friendliness. Apple tries to tell the people who view their webpage that they spend a LOT of effort on their products, and they speak for themselves. That's why we don't need big, long explanations of our products; rather just big photos so you can see it.
